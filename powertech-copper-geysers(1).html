Remember the copper
ande on the stove?

"In every village home, there was a copper vessel sitting on the wood-fired stove."

Every morning, in homes across Karnataka and South India, water was heated in a large copper vessel — the ande — placed directly over a wood-fired stove. As the water boiled, it naturally absorbed copper ions, making it antibacterial, mineral-rich, and gentle on the body.

That water bathed the newborn baby. Washed a pregnant woman's hair. Served the whole family every single day. Nobody called it "health water" — it was simply how water was supposed to be.

Somewhere along the way, we replaced that copper with plastic-lined steel geysers and municipal chemical treatment. The water changed. Many families notice the difference.
